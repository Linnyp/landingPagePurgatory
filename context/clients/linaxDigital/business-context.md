## 1. The Business

- **Business name:** Linax Digital
- **Slug:** linax-digital

- **Business model:** Services-first agency. - **Brand archetype:** The Everyman. Linax Digital presents as a relatable, trustworthy, non-technical partner — not a tech-first disruptor. The agency should feel like someone a local business owner would hire the way they'd hire a trusted contractor.
- **Notes:** Core positioning rests on five pillars: a modern technical foundation (Next.js / React vs. template-based local agency sites), founder-led quality with deliberate scale intent, AI-augmented delivery via custom multi-agent systems, platform-powered services rather than platform-as-product, and deep Southwest Florida local expertise. All of these differentiators must be communicated in plain, non-technical language consistent with the Everyman archetype. Never lead with the technology itself; lead with the outcome it produces for the business owner.

## 2. The Offer

### Primary offer

Four core services for local service businesses:

- **Website** — UX/UI design, development on modern frameworks (Next.js, React, TypeScript, TailwindCSS), hosting, ongoing maintenance, analytics setup, and chatbot integration. Mobile-first, optimized for local search, designed to convert.
- **SEO Foundation** — Keyword research (Ahrefs and similar), on-page mapping, technical SEO, local citations with unified NAP, foundational backlinks, press releases where applicable, and Google Business Profile setup and optimization. Designed as the entry point for local businesses with limited or nonexistent existing SEO; intended to evolve into more comprehensive SEO programs as businesses grow.
- **Ads** — Google Ads (search, display, local services, performance max) and Meta Ads (Facebook, Instagram). Includes ad copywriting, audience segmentation, full active campaign management, ongoing analysis, and monthly performance reporting.
- **Reputation Management** — Flagship done-for-you service and the primary onramp to the operations platform. Every engagement begins with provisioning a dedicated Go High Level sub-account for the client's business. Reputation workflows (review requests, feedback generation, sentiment analysis, automated customer engagement) are configured and actively managed by Linax Digital. Clients receive platform login access and use the CRM and unified inbox to manage all customer conversations in one place.

### Billing & contract terms

- Project fees billed on milestones; monthly subscriptions billed monthly.
- **Website subscription has a 12-month minimum.** Clients who cancel before completing the 12 months are responsible for the full $3,000 lump-sum website fee. Clients who complete the 12-month term and later cancel retain ownership of their domain and all associated assets, except the website and design files themselves. This structure incentivizes long-term retention: continuing past the initial contract is significantly more cost-effective than rebuilding from scratch.
- **SEO, Ads, and Reputation Management are month-to-month.** No long-term lock-in on these services.

### Primary differentiators (from v3 §7)

1. **Modern technical foundation.** Most local SMB agencies still ship template-based sites on dated platforms. Linax Digital builds on Next.js and React, producing faster, more maintainable, more SEO-friendly sites.
2. **Founder-led quality with scale intent.** Clients work directly with a technical founder today, and the agency is structured to grow into specialized teams rather than outsource everything to offshore production as it scales.
3. **AI-augmented delivery.** Research, content, and operational workflows are accelerated by custom multi-agent AI systems, giving clients more output per dollar than traditional agencies of comparable size.
4. **Platform-powered services, not platform-as-product.** Clients get the benefits of a modern operations platform (unified inbox, CRM, automations) as part of services, without the learning curve or setup burden of buying a SaaS tool.
5. **Southwest Florida local expertise.** Deep familiarity with the local market — verticals, seasonality, competitive dynamics — that a remote or national agency cannot match.

### Guarantee / risk reversal

- **Free initial audit + discovery call.** No-commitment entry point for prospective clients to understand their current digital presence and how Linax Digital can help.
- **Ad performance guarantee.** For Ads engagements, clients don't pay management fees if ROAS falls below 2:1.
- **No long-term lock-in on SEO, Ads, or Reputation Management.** All three are month-to-month. The 12-month Website subscription is the only long-term commitment, and it exists to make modern web development affordable for SMBs through subsidized monthly pricing rather than a $3,000 upfront fee.

### À la carte upsells (for existing Reputation Management clients only)

Once a client is onboarded to the operations platform via Reputation Management, additional platform-powered capabilities can be added on. Pricing is quoted per client based on scope. Common upsells include:

- SMS marketing campaigns
- Email marketing campaigns
- Rewards and loyalty systems
- Advanced marketing and operational automations
- Chatbots and conversational AI
- Custom workflow builds

### Current launch status

Founder-led delivery phase. Onboarding initial paying clients across the four core services. Formalizing subcontractor relationships and standardizing service delivery playbooks. Brand and public-facing site not yet live.

## 3. The Audience

- **Primary target customer:** Owners and operators of local service SMBs in Southwest Florida — Lee, Collier, and Charlotte counties. Target verticals include home services (remodeling, garage door, HVAC, roofing, epoxy flooring), marine and charter businesses, specialty contractors, and local retail with a service component. Typically $500K–$5M in annual revenue. Established operators with limited or fragmented digital presence who need a trusted partner to consolidate and modernize their marketing.
- **Secondary audience:** Local service businesses already working with a generalist or template-shop agency but underwhelmed by results — looking for a more technical, hands-on partner who actually owns delivery rather than reselling someone else's work.
- **Most painful problem:** Their digital presence is fragmented, outdated, or leaking leads. Their website doesn't convert, their Google Business Profile is under-optimized, their reviews are stale or scattered, and their lead follow-up is inconsistent. They know they're losing business to better-positioned competitors but don't have the time, in-house staff, or technical know-how to fix it themselves — and they don't trust most local agencies to fix it for them.
- **Current alternative:** Doing marketing themselves (poorly), relying on word-of-mouth and referrals alone, working with a generalist local agency that ships template sites and hits-or-misses on SEO, or paying for a stack of disconnected SaaS tools they never fully use.
- **Desired outcome:** Consistent lead generation, visible trust in their local market, and sustainable growth — supported by a modern website that converts, strong local search visibility, active and accountable paid campaigns, and a managed reputation system that keeps reviews flowing and customer conversations consolidated in one place.
- **Biggest objection to buying:** Cost sensitivity (SMB budgets are tight), skepticism from past bad agency experiences ("I've been burned before"), and uncertainty about ROI timelines on SEO and content. The 12-month Website subscription contract can also raise questions that need to be addressed head-on.
- **Sophistication level:** Warm. They know they need better marketing. Most are aware AI exists but don't understand how it applies to their business. They likely don't know Linax Digital specifically — discovery happens through search, referrals, and local networking.
- **Where they come from:** Google search ("digital marketing agency Cape Coral", "SEO Fort Myers", "local SEO Naples"), Google Business Profile discovery, local networking and chamber events, referrals from existing clients, and targeted Meta and Google ads.

## 5. Visual Preferences

### Brand archetype

**The Everyman.** Linax Digital presents as relatable, grounded, and trustworthy — not as a technical wizard, a disruptor, or a tech-bro. The goal is "one of us" energy: the agency a local business owner would feel comfortable hiring the way they'd hire a trusted contractor. Warmth over polish, clarity over cleverness, plainspoken confidence over jargon.

### Brand persona

Relatable, non-technical, and meets prospects where they are. Small business owners in Southwest Florida don't want to feel dumb, don't want to be talked down to, and don't want to be impressed by jargon they can't decode. The Linax Digital brand should feel like a smart, competent friend or trusted neighbor — the kind of person who explains things plainly because they respect you, not because they think you're simple. Confident but never showy. Knowledgeable but never condescending. The agency version of the guy everyone calls when they need something done right.

### Chosen design direction

**Warm Professional with Hometown Modern elements.** Grounded, warm, and trustworthy — visually confident without being intimidating — with authentic Southwest Florida photography and founder-forward storytelling woven throughout to reinforce local credibility. Editorial magazine sensibility with a warm serif display typeface and a cream-and-clay palette that reads as craftsman, not startup.

### Sites to admire

- [practicepromotions.net](https://practicepromotions.net/) — clean professional-services aesthetic targeted at a specific vertical
- [futuremarketing.com.br](https://www.futuremarketing.com.br/) — modern marketing agency with warm visual identity
- [lumos-marketing.com](https://www.lumos-marketing.com/) — confident agency positioning with human feel

### Sites to avoid

- Overly corporate, stock-photo-heavy local agency sites
- Cookie-cutter local marketing agency templates
- Tech-SaaS aesthetics (Linear, Vercel, Stripe, Framer) — too cold, too "for developers," signals the wrong audience
- Neon or hyper-saturated "dopamine" palettes — too young, too lifestyle, wrong for SMB trust
- Dark-mode-first designs — reads as "tech bro," wrong for non-technical owners
- Neubrutalism and anti-polish aesthetics — too aggressive, will read as unprofessional to the target audience

### Vibe adjectives

Warm, grounded, trustworthy, approachable, competent, neighborly, professional-but-not-stuffy, locally rooted, plainspoken, confident, editorial.

### Visual design system

The full visual design system — color tokens, typography scale, spacing, borders, shadows, components, and animations — is documented in **`styleguide.md`** in this same directory. That file is the source of truth for visual implementation and design tokens. AI agents building or modifying the site must read `styleguide.md` before generating any layout, color, or type decisions.

**Locked decisions reflected in the style guide:**

- **Palette:** Coastal Clay — warm cream base (`#FBF8F3`), soft sand surfaces, warm near-black text, terracotta accent (`#C2552D`), muted sage for success states
- **Typography:** Fraunces for display/headings (warm serif with optical sizing), Inter for body and UI (clean, highly readable sans). Two fonts total.
- **Overall feel:** Warm Professional with editorial magazine sensibility. Light backgrounds throughout, one warm footer section. No dark hero, no dramatic gradients, no tech-SaaS aesthetics.

### Photography direction

Authentic Southwest Florida — real local photography of the founder, real clients (with permission), real local businesses, real SWFL environments. Avoid generic stock photography. Where photography isn't appropriate, use simple custom illustrations over iconography, and simple iconography over complex illustrations. The photography should feel like it was taken by someone who lives here, not commissioned for an out-of-state agency pitch.

### Logo

Draft logo exists. Final logo to be confirmed before site launch. Logo should align with the Coastal Clay palette and Fraunces/Inter type direction documented in `styleguide.md`.

## 6. Page Goals & Constraints

- **Primary conversion goal:** Book a free consultation via embedded Calendly. Every page should have a clear, persistent path to the Calendly CTA.
- **Secondary conversion goal:** Lead capture via contact form (collected through Go High Level), feeding into the Linax Digital CRM for follow-up.
- **Lead magnet:** Free Digital Presence Audit. Delivered as a structured review of the prospect's current website, SEO, GBP, and online reputation. Serves as the low-friction entry point until the self-serve automated report product (in development per v3 §8) ships publicly.
- **Required sections:**
  - Homepage with clear value proposition, services overview, differentiators, and primary CTA
  - Dedicated service pages for each of the four core services (Website, SEO, Ads, Reputation Management) with pricing transparency
  - About / Founder page that establishes technical credibility and the founder-led quality story
  - Case studies / results page (placeholder structure now, populated as results mature)
  - Free Digital Presence Audit landing page with lead capture form
  - Contact / Book a Call page with embedded Calendly
- **Excluded sections:** No standalone "AI services" page — AI is a delivery method woven through every service, not a product. No e-commerce. No client login portal on the marketing site (client login lives inside the Go High Level sub-account).
- **Legal requirements:** Standard privacy policy and terms of service. Cookie consent banner. No industry-specific regulatory requirements anticipated.
- **Accessibility requirements:** WCAG 2.1 AA as the standard. See `styleguide.md` §14 for contrast ratios and focus-state specifications.
- **Integrations / forms:**
  - **Scheduling:** Calendly (embedded for the free consultation flow)
  - **Email capture / CRM:** Go High Level (forms feed directly into the agency sub-account)
  - **Analytics:** Google Analytics 4 + Google Tag Manager
  - **Hosting:** Vercel
  - **Stack:** Next.js, React, TypeScript, TailwindCSS

## 7. Tone & Copy Guidance

> **Writing voice reference:** The full writing voice specification — including reference writers (Vonnegut, Hemingway, Twain, Ogilvy, King, Handley, Jarvis, Godin), distilled sentence/vocabulary/tone/structure rules, before-and-after copy examples, and a pre-submission checklist — is documented in **`writing-voice.md`** in this same directory. Any AI agent writing copy for Linax Digital must load and follow `writing-voice.md`. The summary below is supplementary context; `writing-voice.md` is the authority.

- **Voice adjectives:** Direct, knowledgeable, approachable, plainspoken, no-BS, warm, locally grounded.
- **Formality level:** 2.5 out of 5 — professional but conversational. Speaks like a knowledgeable friend or trusted neighbor, never like a corporate brochure. Easy for non-technical local business owners to follow without ever feeling talked down to.
- **Humor:** Subtle dry wit. Confident but never trying too hard. No memes, no forced casualness, no "fellow kids" energy.
- **Words to avoid:** "Synergy," "leverage" (as a verb in marketing-speak), "disrupt," "guru," "ninja," "rockstar," "revolutionary," "game-changer," "unlock," "supercharge," "next-level," "cutting-edge" (as filler), "solutions" (as filler), "empower," "elevate." Avoid empty hype language and AI-marketing clichés. Avoid technical jargon unless it's immediately translated into plain language. See `writing-voice.md` for the full never-use list including AI-ese tells (delve, tapestry, myriad, seamless, robust, etc.).
- **Existing slogans / taglines:** None. To be defined after site copy is drafted.
- **Key messages to include** (all phrased for the Everyman persona — plain, confident, outcome-focused):
  - **Built to last, not a template.** "Your website is built to work hard and last — not a cookie-cutter template that looks like every other local business in town."
  - **You talk to the person doing the work.** "No account managers, no handoffs, no 'let me check with the team.' You work directly with the person building your marketing."
  - **Modern tools, more work done.** "We use modern tools to get more done in less time without cutting corners. You get the benefit — faster turnarounds, sharper work — without paying for it."
  - **Stop juggling five subscriptions.** "One system handles your reviews, your customer conversations, and your follow-ups. Less software, less headache, one bill."
  - **We're from here.** "We know your customers, your competitors, and your busy season. That's not something a national agency can fake."
  - **Honest pricing.** "Our prices are on the website. No runaround, no 'contact us for a quote' games."
