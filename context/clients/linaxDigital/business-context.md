## 3. The Audience _(required)_

- **Primary target customer:** Owners and operators of small to medium-sized local service businesses (HVAC, plumbing, landscaping, remodeling, marine charters) in Southwest Florida and similar markets — typically 1–30 employees, doing $500K–$5M+ in revenue, who know they need better marketing and are curious about AI but don't have in-house digital or tech staff.
- **Secondary audience:** Local service businesses already working with a generalist agency but underwhelmed by results; looking for a more technical, hands-on partner.
- **Most painful problem:** They're leaving money on the table because their online presence is weak, their lead follow-up is slow or inconsistent, and they have no idea how to leverage AI tools that their competitors will soon be using.
- **Current alternative:** Doing marketing themselves (poorly), relying on word-of-mouth alone, using a generic agency that doesn't understand AI or local SEO deeply, or doing nothing.
- **Desired outcome:** A steady, predictable flow of qualified local leads, a professional online presence that builds trust, and operational workflows that save hours per week through automation — without having to become tech-savvy themselves.
- **Biggest objection to buying:** Cost sensitivity (SMB budgets are tight), skepticism about AI ("is this actually useful or just hype?"), and past bad experiences with agencies that over-promised and under-delivered.
- **Sophistication level:** Warm — they know they need better marketing; most are aware AI is a thing but don't understand how it applies to their business. They likely don't know this specific brand yet.
- **Where they come from:** Word of mouth, local networking, Google search ("digital marketing agency near me", "AI for small business"), LinkedIn outreach, and referrals from existing clients.
