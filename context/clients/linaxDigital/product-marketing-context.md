## Product Overview

**One-liner:** Linax Digital is a founder-led digital marketing agency in Southwest Florida delivering websites, SEO, paid ads, and reputation management to local service businesses — powered by a modern technical foundation and AI-augmented delivery.

**What it does:** A services-first agency offering four core done-for-you services to local service SMBs: custom website design and development, local SEO, Google and Meta ads management, and reputation management via a managed Go High Level platform. Clients get modern marketing infrastructure — websites built on Next.js/React, active campaign management, and a unified CRM/inbox for reviews and customer conversations — handled entirely by the founder. AI-augmented workflows accelerate research, content, and operations internally; clients benefit from faster output and sharper work without needing to understand the technology.

**Product category:** Digital marketing agency for local service businesses. How customers search: "digital marketing agency Cape Coral," "local SEO Fort Myers," "website design Southwest Florida," "reputation management SWFL."

**Product type:** B2B services — combination of one-time project fees and monthly subscriptions. No standalone SaaS product sold to end customers.

**Business model:**
- **Website:** $3,000 lump-sum (5-page custom build) + $25/mo hosting. OR $180/mo subscription (12-month minimum) with hosting, edits, and support included.
- **SEO Foundation:** $600 one-time setup + $300/mo ongoing.
- **Ads:** $300/mo minimum or 10% of ad spend, capped at $2,000/mo management fee.
- **Reputation Management:** $195/mo — includes dedicated Go High Level sub-account, all reputation workflows managed by Linax Digital, and client CRM + unified inbox access.
- À la carte platform upsells (SMS marketing, email campaigns, loyalty programs, automations) available only to existing Reputation Management clients, priced per scope.

## Target Audience

**Target companies:** Local service SMBs in Southwest Florida — Lee, Collier, and Charlotte counties. Target verticals: home services (HVAC, plumbing, roofing, remodeling, epoxy flooring, garage door), marine and charter businesses, specialty contractors, local retail with a service component. Typically $500K–$5M annual revenue. Established operators with limited or fragmented digital presence.

**Decision-makers:** Business owner or operator — typically the same person. Decisions made on gut + referrals + visible results. Budget-conscious but will invest when ROI is clear and trust is established. Secondary influencer: office manager or operations coordinator who researches and champions tools before presenting to the owner.

**Primary use case:** Consolidate and modernize a fragmented digital presence so the phone rings more consistently — with a trusted partner who actually does the work rather than coordinating vendors.

**Jobs to be done:**
- "Get my phone ringing with quality leads without chasing Angi or HomeAdvisor."
- "Make my online presence look as credible as the quality of work I actually deliver."
- "Stop losing leads because nobody follows up fast enough when I'm on a job."
- "Have someone I can trust to handle marketing so I can run my actual business."

**Use cases:**
- HVAC owner rebuilds website + ranks in Google Local Pack + automates review requests via Reputation Management.
- Boat charter company optimizes GBP, runs Meta Ads for peak season bookings, and gets a CRM to track returning guests.
- Legacy remodeling company (25+ years) modernizes digital presence to compete with younger, more digital-savvy competitors.
- Solo service operator (cleaning, detailing, personal training) gets a professional website and booking system for the first time.
- Multi-location franchise operator deploys per-location managed marketing with rollup reporting.

## Problems & Pain Points

**Core problem:** Local service business owners have a fragmented, outdated, or underperforming digital presence that leaks leads. Their website doesn't convert, their Google Business Profile is under-optimized, their reviews are stale or scattered, and their lead follow-up is inconsistent. They know it's costing them business. They don't have time to fix it, and they don't trust most agencies to fix it for them.

**Why current alternatives fall short:**
- **DIY marketing:** Sporadic, inconsistent, outside their skillset. A nephew who posts to Facebook twice a month isn't a marketing strategy.
- **Generalist local agencies:** Template sites, unclear deliverables, junior execution, no data transparency, no AI capability, and often outsource to the same offshore production houses.
- **National SEO platforms / DIY tools (BrightLocal, Semrush):** Require the owner to operate them. That's exactly the time they don't have.
- **Disconnected SaaS stack:** Paying for 4–6 tools (separate website CMS, email platform, review tool, scheduling software, texting app) they never fully use and can't connect.
- **Angi / HomeAdvisor / shared-lead platforms:** Expensive and shared with 3–5 competitors. Not owned traffic.

**What it costs them:** Lost jobs to better-positioned competitors, slow seasons with no pipeline, hours per week manually chasing reviews and following up on leads, overpaying for a stack of tools that don't talk to each other.

**Emotional tension:** "I've been burned before. I know I need this but I don't know who to trust and I can't afford to waste money again." Layered under that: "I don't want to feel stupid in front of someone who talks in jargon I can't follow."

## Competitive Landscape

**Direct competitors — generalist local marketing agencies in SWFL:**
Fall short because they ship template sites on dated platforms (WordPress, Squarespace), don't offer AI-augmented delivery, rely on junior or offshore production for execution, hide pricing, and use account managers who aren't the people doing the work. The prospect already has one — and it's underperforming.

**Secondary competitors — national platforms / franchise marketing agencies:**
Fall short because they have no local SWFL market expertise, no founder-direct accountability, and often charge premium prices for cookie-cutter output. They also can't offer the platform-powered Reputation Management service that's native to Linax Digital's stack.

**Indirect competitors — doing nothing / word of mouth only:**
The single biggest competitor for most prospects. It "works" just enough to rationalize not changing. Falls short because it's not scalable, is vulnerable to market shifts (post-Hurricane Ian recovery tapering off), and leaves the business invisible to customers who search rather than ask for referrals.

**Key competitive moat:**
1. Modern technical foundation (Next.js/React) — most local agency competitors still ship WordPress template sites.
2. Founder-led delivery — the person selling is the person building. No handoffs.
3. AI-augmented delivery — faster turnarounds, sharper research, more output per dollar than agencies of comparable size.
4. Platform-powered services — Reputation Management clients get a managed Go High Level sub-account as part of the service. Unified inbox + CRM + review workflows — not a SaaS product they have to learn, a service that's run for them.
5. SWFL local expertise — verticals, seasonality, competitive dynamics. Cannot be faked by a remote or national agency.

## Switching Dynamics

**Push (frustrations driving them away from current solution):**
- Paying an agency monthly with unclear ROI and no reporting transparency.
- Feeling like a small account at a large agency — slow responses, account manager turnover.
- Results plateau or stagnate after initial launch enthusiasm.
- Still manually chasing reviews and following up on leads after months of "marketing."

**Pull (what attracts them to Linax Digital):**
- Founder-direct accountability — one person, one phone number, one accountable partner.
- Transparent pricing published on the website — unusual in the local agency market.
- Free Digital Presence Audit as a no-risk entry point.
- The ROAS guarantee on Ads — skin in the game.
- Platform-powered Reputation Management that replaces 2–3 disconnected tools.

**Habit (what keeps them stuck with current approach):**
- Existing agency relationship — switching feels like work and risk.
- "At least I know what I'm getting" inertia.
- Skepticism from past failures — easier to stay than risk another disappointment.
- Word-of-mouth working "well enough" to rationalize inaction.

**Anxiety (what worries them about switching):**
- "What if this doesn't work either?" (past agency trauma)
- "Am I locked into something I can't get out of?"
- "Will I have to learn a bunch of new software?"
- "What happens to my website/data if I cancel?"
- "What does 2:1 ROAS actually mean for my business?"
